We were given the freedom to choose our own topic and a corresponding color of significance for an assignment involving the creation of a coffee table book. Opting for scuba diving as my subject, I decided to use various tints and shades of teal blue to symbolize the connection with the sea and the undersea world. Creating a cohesive layout for the booklet posed both a comprehensible yet challenging task to minimize overall design and content. Within the book, I delved into essential information one should know before embarking on a diving journey. This project provided an opportunity to improve my skills in Photoshop and Illustrator, while also expanding my knowledge of the InDesign program.

This digital illustration depicts the renowned Lotus Gate in Jaipur, celebrated for its exquisite detailing and design. While the original colors had faded over time, I chose to breathe new life into the artwork by infusing vibrant hues. Every minute detail and intricate design were meticulously observed and illustrated, recognizing the unique significance of each element. Dedicated to Lord Shiva and Parvati, the gate symbolizes the summer season, prompting the incorporation of bright colors reminiscent of sunshine. The process of creating this illustration was immensely enjoyable, offering insights into the importance of even the smallest design elements.

The matchbox poster design is inspired by the Rococo period, an 18th-century artistic movement known for its intricate style. Drawing from Rococo aesthetics, the poster elegantly combines ornate detailing, asymmetry, and delicate ornamentation to create a visually stunning composition. Elaborate swirls and graceful curves pay tribute to the opulence of the Rococo era, while a carefully chosen pastel color palette evokes elegance and sophistication. This modern interpretation seamlessly blends historical inspiration with contemporary flair, resulting in a captivating piece that honors the timeless beauty of Rococo design principles.

We had to select a suitable product that complements the font we created based on graph paper alphabets and then design its packaging. We had the freedom to choose another font for additional design and essential text. I opted to create packaging for the "Bath and Body Works" aromatherapy product line, using the Raleway font for necessary text alongside the font I designed for the brand name. The packaging was kept simple, featuring minimal illustrations representing the aromatherapy ingredients. This assignment was fascinating and provided insights into designing a typeface and applying it to a practical project.
